Struthiopteris fallax surfing the temperature wave

Struthiopteris fallax is Iceland’s very own hot-spring-loving fern! Found only at Deildartunguhver, this tiny plant thrives in the warm, steamy soil near Iceland’s famous hot spring. Despite its cozy habitat, it's considered endangered in Iceland, with a very limited population.

This project maps the micro-temperature landscape around Deildartunguhver, showing how S. fallax “surfs” the geothermal heat. Data collected in 2021 from a 100 m² grid records each plant’s location and temperature to reveal how this unique fern survives its intense, heated home.
